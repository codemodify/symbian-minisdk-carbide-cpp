...OS Types

(o) General purpose
    Oriented to a wide are of usage. These kind of OSes have in common the kernel architecutre,
    the difference being the manufacturer and the implementation.
    Ex: xBSD, Mac OS, Linux, Windows.

(o) Real time
    Oriented to a speciffic area of the industry. The kernel is especially designed to meet
    real-time requirements which is basically a reflection of the hardware they operate with.
    Ex: QNX, Nucleus, μITRON

(o) Smartphone oriented
    These kind of OSes reprezent a big challenge and interest because they basically
    are a mix of RealTime+General purpose OSes. Their kernel architecture must incorporate
    management approches taken from both branches. This is because the very nature of where
    they are used and with what they have deal with.
    Ex: Symbian OS, iPhone OS, Android, Windows Mobile.
